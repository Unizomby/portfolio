How It's Calculated

Annual Hours Saved

(Current Hrs/Unit − Proposed Hrs/Unit) × Annual Volume

Annual Gross Savings

Annual Hours Saved × Labor Rate

Net Annual Cash Flow

(Gross Savings × Realization Rate) − Annual OpEx

Year 1 applies the Year 1 Realization % as a ramp-up factor. Year 0 = −Implementation Cost.

Net Present Value (NPV)

Σ [ Cash Flow(yr) / (1 + Discount Rate)^yr ] for yr = 0 … Horizon

A positive NPV means the project returns more than the cost of capital over the horizon.

ROI (Simple)

(Total Net Benefits − Implementation Cost) / Implementation Cost

This is a simple, undiscounted ROI. For a time-value-adjusted measure, compare NPV to the initial investment.

Deterministic NPV ?NPV calculated using the exact base-case input values — no randomness. Use as a reference to compare against the simulation mean and median.
—
Mean NPV ?Average NPV across all simulation iterations. In a symmetric model this should be close to the deterministic NPV and P50.
—
P50 NPV ?50th percentile (median) — half of simulated scenarios fall below this value. In a well-calibrated model, this should be close to the deterministic NPV.
—
P10 NPV ?10th percentile — 10% of scenarios produced an NPV below this value. Represents a pessimistic but plausible outcome for downside planning.
—
P90 NPV ?90th percentile — only 10% of scenarios produced a higher NPV. Represents an optimistic but plausible upside outcome.
—
Prob. Negative NPV ?Share of scenarios where NPV < 0. Above 20–30% warrants careful review of key assumptions before committing to the investment.
—
Prob. Positive Return ?Percentage of scenarios where NPV ≥ 0 — the complement of Prob. Negative NPV. Higher is better; aim for 80%+ for a defensible business case.
